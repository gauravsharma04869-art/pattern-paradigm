## 1. Predictive Processing & Bayesian Brain

**The Core Discovery: The brain doesn't process reality — it *predicts* it.**

The Bayesian Brain / Predictive Processing framework is the closest modern science has come to the Trika understanding of consciousness. Key findings:

### What it says:
- The brain maintains a **generative model** (a set of probabilistic beliefs) about the world
- It *predicts* sensory input before it arrives
- **Prediction error** = the difference between prediction and actual input
- The brain updates its model to minimize prediction error
- Perception is "controlled hallucination" — we don't see reality, we see our *predictions about reality*

### Direct Pattern Paradigm support:
- Beliefs are literally *patterns encoded in neural networks*
- These patterns *generate expectations* that shape perception
- A person with a "I'm sick" pattern will perceive and interpret everything through that filter
- The body follows the brain's predictions — if the brain predicts illness, the body produces illness

| Paper | Source | Year | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bayesian brain theory: computational neuroscience of belief | ScienceDirect | 2024 | Formal mathematical model of how beliefs are encoded and updated as probabilistic neural patterns |
| Dynamic Predictive Coding: hierarchical sequence learning | PLOS Comp Bio | 2024 | Brain learns hierarchical temporal patterns — higher levels modulate lower-level pattern transitions |
| Predictive learning shapes representational geometry of human brain | Nature (MEG study) | 2024 | Brain aligns its representational geometry to match statistical structure of sensory inputs |

| Sculpting new visual categories into the human brain | Nature / PMC | 2024 | **Direct neurofeedback created new neural categories** without experience — the brain can be *re-patterned* directly |

### The Ultimate Implication:
If perception is controlled hallucination, then **what we call "reality" is always a pattern-interpretation.** The Trika claim that "the world is a manifestation of consciousness" is no longer metaphysical — it's computationally precise.

## 2. Placebo Effect: Belief Changes Biology

**The Core Discovery: What you *expect* to happen literally changes your physiology.**

The placebo effect is not "all in your head" — it's the *body following the brain's predictions*.

### Key Research:

| Paper | Source | Year | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Neural circuit basis of placebo pain relief | Nature | 2024 | **Identified the exact neural circuit** (rACC→Pn pathway) that mediates placebo analgesia. Opened the door for drug/neurostimulation targeting |
| Placebo treatment affects brain systems — not nociceptive pain | Nature Comms | 2024 | Placebo affects *affective and cognitive* pain processing, not raw nociception — meaning it changes *what pain means*, not just sensation |
| Reverse-engineering placebo analgesia | Cell / Wang Lab | 2024 | **Engineered robust placebo effect in mice** using context + circuit activation. First time placebo was created through specific neural manipulation |
| Open-Label Placebo Injection for Chronic Back Pain | JAMA / PubMed | 2024 | Non-deceptive placebo (honestly labeled) reduced chronic back pain for 1 month with benefits lasting 1 year. Engaged prefrontal-brainstem pain pathways |
| Differential neural activity predicts long-term stability of placebo/nocebo effects | Scientific Reports | 2024 | **Expectation effects can self-reinforce** — they don't extinguish naturally because they create feedback loops |
| Neuroplasticity Placebo Theory | Frontiers in Psychiatry | 2024 | **Placebo stimulates neuroplasticity** in fronto-limbic areas. Placebo is an active treatment, not an inert one |
| Enacting the mind/body connection: self-induced placebo mechanisms | Humanities & Soc Sci Comms | 2024 | Framework for **self-inducing placebo effects** through mental imagery, somatic focusing, and perceived control |
| Mapping expectancy-based appetitive placebo effects onto the brain | Nature Comms | 2024 | Placebo suggestions about hunger changed *how the brain weighed food choices* — drifted toward or away from tastiness/healthiness |

### Pattern Paradigm Connection:
- Placebo is not magic — it's **prediction in action**
- Your beliefs (patterns) tell your body what state to be in
- The body is *obedient* to the pattern, not to external reality
- This is why people who've "tried everything" don't get better — their underlying *pattern* hasn't changed
- Open-label placebo works because even *knowing* it's placebo doesn't override the power of the healing *ritual and meaning*

## 3. Epigenetics: Environment Patterns Gene Expression

**The Core Discovery: Your environment and behavior change which genes are expressed. These changes can be inherited.**

Epigenetics is the study of how patterns *outside* DNA control DNA expression. It's the biological mechanism of the Pattern Paradigm.

### Key Research:

| Paper | Source | Year | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oxytocin receptor epigenetics shaped by early life environment | Pediatric Research | 2024 | Early life environment literally changes the methylation of oxytocin receptor genes — affecting social behavior for life |
| Environmental exposures influence multigenerational epigenetic transmission | Clinical Epigenetics | 2024 | Environmental impacts on epigenetics can be **transmitted to grandchildren** — your parents' patterns become your biology |
| Maternal microbiome programs offspring behavior via hippocampal DNA methylation | bioRxiv | 2024 | **Maternal gut microbiome alone** influenced offspring anxiety, activity, and body weight through epigenetic changes in the hippocampus |
| Mother adversity shapes mother-child methylation similarity | Clinical Epigenetics | 2024 | Mother's adverse experiences correlated with **shared methylation patterns** between mother and child |
| PTSD-associated DNA methylation sites identified | Genome Medicine | 2024 | 11 CpG sites associated with PTSD — trauma leaves epigenetic marks that can be measured |
| Histone serotonylation in stress and antidepressant response | Nature Comms | 2024 | **Serotonin epigenetically modifies histones** — stress changes gene expression through a novel mechanism |
| Exercise trains the brain through epigenetic modifications | Int J Mol Sci | 2024 | Physical exercise promotes hypomethylation of BDNF promoter — enhancing neuroplasticity |
| Gut microbiome mediates environmental effects on host DNA methylation | BMC Genomics | 2024 | Gut microbiome *mediates the effect of environment on gene methylation* — microbes are translators of environmental patterns |

### Pattern Paradigm Connection:
- Epigenetics is the **mechanism by which patterns become biology**
- A pattern held long enough (stress, trauma, belief) becomes methylated into gene expression
- This is not permanent — methylation patterns can change
- **Changing the pattern changes the biology** — this is the scientific basis for what Trika called "mala"

## 4. Neuroplasticity: Patterns Rewire the Brain

**The Core Discovery: The brain continues to rewire itself throughout life — for better or worse.**

### Key Research:

| Paper | Source | Year | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| The neuroplastic brain: current breakthroughs | Brain Research | 2025 | Comprehensive review: neuroplasticity continues lifespan, supports learning, recovery, and **can be adaptive OR maladaptive** |
| Mind-body intervention: meditation + reconceptualization + open-label placebo | Comms Biology (Nature) | 2025 | 7-day retreat: **decreased DMN connectivity, increased BDNF, modulated opioid pathways, enhanced neuroplasticity** |
| Mindfulness-Based Interventions and Neuroplasticity | Mindfulness (Springer) | 2025 | MBIs associated with plasticity in DMN, salience network, and central executive network — corresponding to improved emotion regulation |
| Confirmation bias through selective readout — NOT selective encoding | Nature Comms | 2025 | **Confirmation bias operates by biasing the *readout* of information, not its encoding.** Room for strategic intervention is substantial |
| Neurogenetic phenotypes of learning-dependent plasticity | Comms Biology | 2025 | Genetic expression in brain regions links to learning-dependent changes in microstructure and functional connectivity |
| REBUS → REBAS: psychedelics relax and revise beliefs | Scientific Reports | 2025 | Psilocybin decreases confidence in negative self-beliefs through increased neural entropy — **patterns can be "loosened"** |

### Pattern Paradigm Connection:
- Neuroplasticity is the *mechanism* for pattern change at the biological level
- The same plasticity that locks maladaptive patterns in place can *unlock* them
- The key insight: **patterns persist because they create self-reinforcing feedback loops** — not because they're permanent
- Mindfulness, reconceptualization, and even placebo all work through the same plasticity mechanisms

## 5. The Default Mode Network (DMN) & Self-Pattern

**The Core Discovery: The sense of a separate "self" is a brain network pattern — not a fixed entity.**

- The DMN is active when you're not focused on external tasks — it's the "narrative self" network
- Meditation *decreases* DMN connectivity (Nature Comms Biology, 2025)
- Psychedelics *disrupt* DMN integration (REBUS model)
- The DMN is where the *pattern of self* lives

This directly maps to Trika's understanding: the sense of being a separate, limited self (*purusa/anava mala*) is a *pattern* — not a fixed reality.
